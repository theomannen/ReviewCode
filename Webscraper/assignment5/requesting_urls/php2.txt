<p><b>Hurricane Gonzalo</b> was the second <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a>, after <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Fay" title="Hurricane Fay">Hurricane Fay</a>, to directly strike the island of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> in a one-week time frame in October 2014, and was the first <a href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_4" class="mw-redirect" title="SSHS">Category<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>4 Atlantic hurricane</a> since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ophelia_(2011)" title="Hurricane Ophelia (2011)">Hurricane Ophelia</a> in <a href="/wiki/2011_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2011 Atlantic hurricane season">2011</a>. At the time, it was the strongest hurricane in the Atlantic since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Igor" title="Hurricane Igor">Igor</a> in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Gonzalo struck Bermuda less than a week after the surprisingly fierce <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Fay_(2014)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hurricane Fay (2014)">Hurricane Fay</a>; 2014 was the first season in recorded history to feature two hurricane landfalls in Bermuda. A powerful <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane" title="Atlantic hurricane">Atlantic tropical cyclone</a> that wrought destruction in the <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands" title="Leeward Islands">Leeward Islands</a> and Bermuda, Gonzalo was the seventh named storm, sixth and final hurricane and only the second major hurricane of the below-average <a href="/wiki/2014_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2014 Atlantic hurricane season">2014 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. The storm formed from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> on October 12, while located east of the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>. It made landfall on <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">Antigua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Collectivity_of_Saint_Martin" title="Collectivity of Saint Martin">Saint Martin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Anguilla" title="Anguilla">Anguilla</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Saffir–Simpson hurricane wind scale">Category<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>1 hurricane</a>, causing damage on those and nearby islands. <a href="/wiki/Antigua_and_Barbuda" title="Antigua and Barbuda">Antigua and Barbuda</a> sustained US$40<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>million in losses, and boats were abundantly damaged or destroyed throughout the northern Leeward Islands. The storm killed three people on Saint Martin and <a href="/wiki/Saint_Barth%C3%A9lemy" title="Saint Barthélemy">Saint Barthélemy</a>. Gonzalo tracked northwestward as it intensified into a major hurricane. <a href="/wiki/Eyewall_replacement_cycle" title="Eyewall replacement cycle">Eyewall replacement cycles</a> led to fluctuations in the hurricane's structure and intensity, but on October 16, Gonzalo peaked with maximum sustained winds of 145 mph (230 km/h).
</p><p>After Hurricane Fay caused extensive power outages on the island just days before, residents of Bermuda were forced to complete preparations for Gonzalo in haste. Banks, businesses, schools, and government offices closed in advance of the storm, while the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> ship <a href="/wiki/HMS_Argyll_(F231)" title="HMS Argyll (F231)">HMS <i>Argyll</i></a> left its post in the Caribbean to provide Bermuda with emergency assistance. The cyclone gradually weakened before crossing directly over central Bermuda at Category<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>2 strength around 00:30 <a href="/wiki/Universal_Coordinated_Time" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Coordinated Time">UTC</a> on October 18. Gonzalo battered the island with wind gusts as high as 144&#160;mph (232&#160;km/h), downing hundreds of trees and creating widespread roof damage. At the height of the storm, about 31,000 out of 36,000 total electricity customers were without power; service was not fully restored until early November. Many roads were impassable immediately following the hurricane, and in many cases the damage done by Gonzalo was indistinguishable from that of Fay. <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_Regiment" class="mw-redirect" title="Bermuda Regiment">Bermuda Regiment</a> soldiers and sailors from the <i>Argyll</i> took part in initial cleanup and repairs on the territory, and preliminary assessments revealed that the storm did not compare to the devastation of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Fabian" title="Hurricane Fabian">Hurricane Fabian</a> in 2003. Catastrophe modelling firms estimated that Bermuda suffered at least $200<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>million in insured losses, and despite the heavy disruptions, no deaths or serious injuries were reported there.
</p><p>Departing Bermuda, Gonzalo accelerated toward the waters of the North Atlantic, passing close to southeastern <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a> before becoming <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical</a> on October 19. Gusty winds and bands of heavy rain in the southeastern <a href="/wiki/Avalon_Peninsula" title="Avalon Peninsula">Avalon Peninsula</a> engendered minor flooding and power outages. A large storm system involving the remnants of Gonzalo battered the <a href="/wiki/British_Isles" title="British Isles">British Isles</a> and central Europe on October 21, killing three people in the United Kingdom and severely hindering transportation. The system later played a role in triggering torrential rains over the <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkans</a>, which resulted in severe flooding in Greece and Bulgaria.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Gonzalo_2014_track.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Gonzalo_2014_track.png/250px-Gonzalo_2014_track.png" decoding="async" width="250" height="248" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Gonzalo_2014_track.png/375px-Gonzalo_2014_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Gonzalo_2014_track.png/500px-Gonzalo_2014_track.png 2x" data-file-width="2700" data-file-height="2683" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Gonzalo_2014_track.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Map plotting the track and the intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div></div></div>
<p>Hurricane Gonzalo originated in a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> that emerged from the western coast of Africa on October<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>4 and trekked across the Atlantic. Despite an attendant expanse of clouds and thunderstorms, hostile winds from an <a href="/wiki/Cold-core_low" title="Cold-core low">upper-level trough</a> hindered <a href="/wiki/Cyclogenesis" title="Cyclogenesis">cyclogenesis</a>. On October 10, after encountering an eastward-propagating <a href="/wiki/Kelvin_wave" title="Kelvin wave">Kelvin wave</a>, the system developed more concentrated <a href="/wiki/Convection_(meteorology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Convection (meteorology)">convection</a>. With conditions increasingly favorable for further development, the disturbance gradually became better organized, forming a small <a href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" class="mw-redirect" title="Low pressure area">low pressure area</a> by October 11. In turn, a tropical depression formed at 00:00<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span><a href="/wiki/Universal_Coordinated_Time" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Coordinated Time">UTC</a> on October 12, located approximately 390 mi (630 km) east of the <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands" title="Leeward Islands">Leeward Islands</a>. Continued maturation amid warm waters and low <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a> yielded Tropical Storm Gonzalo 12 hours later.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Operationally, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> (NHC) did not begin issuing advisories on the cyclone until midday on October 12, after a <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" class="mw-redirect" title="Hurricane Hunters">Hurricane Hunter</a> flight into the system reported <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">surface winds</a> of 40 mph (65 km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-disc1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-disc1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A powerful <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a> over the central Atlantic steered the nascent storm westward toward the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Thunderstorm activity was strong near the center, although initially somewhat disorganized and asymmetric.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> However, a tight inner core soon took shape, and Gonzalo began to quickly intensify throughout the day on October 12. Following the appearance of an <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye feature</a> early the next day, the storm was upgraded to a <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Saffir–Simpson hurricane wind scale">Category<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>1 hurricane</a> while located near <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">Antigua</a> in the eastern Caribbean. Shortly thereafter, the storm passed directly over the island.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The hurricane later struck <a href="/wiki/Saint_Martin_(island)" title="Saint Martin (island)">Saint Martin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anguilla" title="Anguilla">Anguilla</a>, and skirted just north of the <a href="/wiki/British_Virgin_Islands" title="British Virgin Islands">British Virgin Islands</a> while continuing to intensify.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> By that time, it was headed toward the northwest around the periphery of the aforementioned ridge.<sup id="cite_ref-disc11_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-disc11-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Gonzalo_Oct_17_2014_1515Z.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Gonzalo_Oct_17_2014_1515Z.jpg/220px-Gonzalo_Oct_17_2014_1515Z.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="240" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Gonzalo_Oct_17_2014_1515Z.jpg/330px-Gonzalo_Oct_17_2014_1515Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Gonzalo_Oct_17_2014_1515Z.jpg/440px-Gonzalo_Oct_17_2014_1515Z.jpg 2x" data-file-width="7340" data-file-height="8000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Gonzalo_Oct_17_2014_1515Z.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hurricane Gonzalo approaching <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> on October 17</div></div></div>
<p>On October 14, the eye contracted to a diameter of 17 mi (28 km) and improved its satellite presentation.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> At 18:00<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>UTC, Gonzalo strengthened to a Category<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>3 major hurricane while located about 170 mi (270 km) north of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Six hours later it became the first Category<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>4 hurricane in the Atlantic basin since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ophelia_(2011)" title="Hurricane Ophelia (2011)">Hurricane Ophelia</a> in 2011. Subsequently, a concentric eyewall structure indicated an imminent <a href="/wiki/Eyewall_replacement_cycle" title="Eyewall replacement cycle">eyewall replacement cycle</a>, with the inner feature "about as small as it can get" according to the NHC.<sup id="cite_ref-disc13_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-disc13-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The eyewall replacement cycle briefly disrupted the storm's core, causing Gonzalo to weaken slightly,<sup id="cite_ref-disc14_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-disc14-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> but upon completion on the evening of October 15, the system stabilized and resumed intensification.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> While moving northward early the next day, Gonzalo reached its peak intensity with winds of 145 mph (230 km/h) and a <a href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" class="mw-redirect" title="Barometric pressure">barometric pressure</a> of 940 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (<a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">hPa</a>; 27.76 <a href="/wiki/Inches_of_Mercury" class="mw-redirect" title="Inches of Mercury">inHg</a>). By evening it had turned north-northeastward, ahead of an advancing <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> over the eastern United States and in the wake of the receding ridge.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From the evening of October 16 through the next morning, the hurricane experienced further internal fluctuations as it approached Bermuda from the south-southwest. Concurrently, the storm began to weaken.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In particular, cloud tops around the hurricane warmed, and convection became less uniform, likely attributable to cooler waters and heightened wind shear.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The hurricane was downgraded to Category<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>2 as the northern eyewall crossed Bermuda,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and about 00:30<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>UTC on October 18, the center of circulation passed directly over the island, signalling an official <a href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a>. Along with Hurricane Fay, which struck Bermuda on October 12, this represented the first recorded instance of two hurricanes making landfall on the island within the same season.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Moving away from Bermuda, the hurricane continued to degrade,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> but showed signs of increased organization later on October 18.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> As Gonzalo accelerated northeastward at over 50 mph (85 km/h),<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> it passed about 50 miles (85 km) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Newfoundland and Labrador">Newfoundland</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Avalon_Peninsula" title="Avalon Peninsula">Avalon Peninsula</a> early on October 19.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In spite of sea surface temperatures below 50&#160;°F (10&#160;°C), the storm was able to maintain a <a href="/wiki/Central_dense_overcast" title="Central dense overcast">central dense overcast</a> and deep warm core.<sup id="cite_ref-disc29_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-disc29-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> By 18:00<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>UTC, Gonzalo had finally succumbed to the cold environment and intense wind shear, completing its transition into a strong <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a> about 460&#160;mi (740&#160;km) northeast of <a href="/wiki/Cape_Race" title="Cape Race">Cape Race</a>, Newfoundland. Gonzalo's remnants sped east-northeastward until a <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">frontal boundary</a> absorbed it on October 20, to the south-southwest of <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>. The resultant system brought stormy weather to the <a href="/wiki/British_Isles" title="British Isles">British Isles</a> and parts of mainland Europe on October 21,<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and later contributed to the formation of a large <a href="/wiki/Cut_off_low" class="mw-redirect" title="Cut off low">cut-off low</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Gonzalo_Viewed_From_the_International_Space_Station.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Hurricane_Gonzalo_Viewed_From_the_International_Space_Station.jpg/250px-Hurricane_Gonzalo_Viewed_From_the_International_Space_Station.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="166" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Hurricane_Gonzalo_Viewed_From_the_International_Space_Station.jpg/375px-Hurricane_Gonzalo_Viewed_From_the_International_Space_Station.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Hurricane_Gonzalo_Viewed_From_the_International_Space_Station.jpg/500px-Hurricane_Gonzalo_Viewed_From_the_International_Space_Station.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4284" data-file-height="2844" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Gonzalo_Viewed_From_the_International_Space_Station.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hurricane Gonzalo approaching Bermuda on October 17, as viewed from the International Space Station</div></div></div>
<p>When Gonzalo first formed, various governments across the eastern Caribbean issued <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">tropical cyclone warnings and watches</a>, extending from <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</a> to the coast of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. As the storm was strengthening and moving through the region, a hurricane warning was issued for the British Virgin Islands, Anguilla, and Saint Martin.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Several major cruise lines altered their itineraries to avoid the storm.<sup id="cite_ref-twc_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twc-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Late on October 14, while Gonzalo was still about 700 miles (1,130 km) to the south, the <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_Weather_Service" title="Bermuda Weather Service">Bermuda Weather Service</a> issued a hurricane watch for the island. The watch was upgraded to a warning the next day.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Having been affected by the unexpectedly destructive <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Fay_(2014)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hurricane Fay (2014)">Hurricane Fay</a> just days earlier, residents quickly began preparing for Gonzalo by obtaining emergency supplies and expediting cleanup efforts from the previous storm.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Premier_of_Bermuda" class="mw-redirect" title="Premier of Bermuda">Premier of Bermuda</a> <a href="/wiki/Michael_Dunkley" title="Michael Dunkley">Michael Dunkley</a> advised citizens to complete most preparations by the afternoon of October 16.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Banks and businesses began closing that day,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> while schools and government offices were closed by October 17.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> One school functioned as a storm shelter,<sup id="cite_ref-causeway_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-causeway-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> and 66 people ultimately sought refuge there.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Bus and ferry services were suspended on the evening of October 16.<sup id="cite_ref-causeway_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-causeway-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Bermuda's only daily newspaper, the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Royal_Gazette_(Bermuda)" title="The Royal Gazette (Bermuda)">Royal Gazette</a></i>, did not go to print on October 17, but distributed the next day's edition for free.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_Regiment" class="mw-redirect" title="Bermuda Regiment">Bermuda Regiment</a> soldiers were stationed at various points to support emergency response crews and ensure the safety of the public.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Caribbean Electric Utility Services Corporation (CARILEC) sent linemen to assist the Bermuda Electric Light Company (BELCO) crews in the event of power outages from Gonzalo. Still completing restoration work after massive outages from Fay, BELCO stationed vehicles and supplies at strategic points on the island to prepare for the next hurricane.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> On the morning of October 16, BELCO switched its focus from Fay to Gonzalo, leaving around 1,500 households without power; the remaining affected customers were asked to refrain from calling to report outages.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Ahead of the storm, the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> frigate <a href="/wiki/HMS_Argyll_(F231)" title="HMS Argyll (F231)">HMS <i>Argyll</i></a> sailed from the Caribbean to provide Bermuda with emergency assistance.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Public beaches were closed as hurricane swells began to build, and the decision was made to shut down <a href="/wiki/The_Causeway,_Bermuda" title="The Causeway, Bermuda">the Causeway</a> early on October 17, based on forecasts of long-duration severe winds.<sup id="cite_ref-causeway_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-causeway-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, <a href="/wiki/L.F._Wade_International_Airport" title="L.F. Wade International Airport">L.F.&#160;Wade International Airport</a> closed prior to the storm's onslaught,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> accounting for 62 canceled commercial flights. Three cruise ships were diverted from the island.<sup id="cite_ref-cruise_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cruise-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_and_aftermath">Impact and aftermath</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Caribbean">Caribbean</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Gonzalo_over_Leeward_Islands.gif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Gonzalo_over_Leeward_Islands.gif/250px-Gonzalo_over_Leeward_Islands.gif" decoding="async" width="250" height="167" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Gonzalo_over_Leeward_Islands.gif/375px-Gonzalo_over_Leeward_Islands.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/ee/Gonzalo_over_Leeward_Islands.gif 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="333" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Gonzalo_over_Leeward_Islands.gif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Infrared satellite loop of Gonzalo moving over the <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands" title="Leeward Islands">Leeward Islands</a> on October 13</div></div></div>
<p>While moving through the northeastern Leeward Islands as a fledgling hurricane, Gonzalo produced sustained winds of 77&#160;mph (124&#160;km/h) on Antigua, with gusts to 90&#160;mph (140&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-aab_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aab-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Roads were blocked by uprooted trees, while numerous buildings, including several schools, received significant damage.<sup id="cite_ref-twc_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twc-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-aab_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aab-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Gonzalo caused an island-wide power outage,<sup id="cite_ref-twc_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twc-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> and its storm surge damaged boats.<sup id="cite_ref-aab_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aab-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Schools and businesses were forced to close, and four emergency shelters opened to storm victims.<sup id="cite_ref-twc_19-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twc-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Debris and flooding forced <a href="/wiki/V._C._Bird_International_Airport" title="V. C. Bird International Airport">V. C. Bird International Airport</a> to delay its return to operation after closing as a precaution.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Numerous homes sustained damage, largely to their roofs, with the <a href="/wiki/Saint_George_Parish,_Antigua_and_Barbuda" title="Saint George Parish, Antigua and Barbuda">Saint George Parish</a> suffering the greatest losses.<sup id="cite_ref-AntiguaDamage_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AntiguaDamage-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Farming communities and a variety of crops were severely impacted,<sup id="cite_ref-aab_33-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aab-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> which sparked concerns of imminent produce shortages.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Some growers lost their entire banana crops.<sup id="cite_ref-aab_33-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aab-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Barbuda" title="Barbuda">Barbuda</a> experienced gusts as high as 70 mph (113 km/h),<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> as well as radar-estimated rainfall of up to 6&#160;in (150&#160;mm).<sup id="cite_ref-aab_33-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aab-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Although Barbuda was subject to less widespread devastation than its twin island, there were still snapped tree limbs, reports of flooding, and disruptions to utility services.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Damages to residences, government buildings, and agriculture on Antigua contributed to <a href="/wiki/Antigua_and_Barbuda" title="Antigua and Barbuda">Antigua and Barbuda</a>'s national storm cost of around US$40<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>million,<sup id="cite_ref-aab_33-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aab-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AntiguaDamage_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AntiguaDamage-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> which also includes compensation to <a href="/wiki/American_Airlines" title="American Airlines">American Airlines</a> for not meeting departing passenger quotas during the hurricane.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Numerous individuals were treated for minor storm-related injuries, none of them life-threatening.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Further west, Gonzalo caused minor power outages on <a href="/wiki/Saint_Kitts_and_Nevis" title="Saint Kitts and Nevis">Saint Kitts and Nevis</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-ccrif_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccrif-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> while a general 1–2&#160;in (25–51&#160;mm) of rain accompanied wind gusts to 58&#160;mph (93&#160;km/h) on Guadeloupe.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Orient_Beach_after_Hurricane_Gonzalo,_St_Martin.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Orient_Beach_after_Hurricane_Gonzalo%2C_St_Martin.jpg/220px-Orient_Beach_after_Hurricane_Gonzalo%2C_St_Martin.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Orient_Beach_after_Hurricane_Gonzalo%2C_St_Martin.jpg/330px-Orient_Beach_after_Hurricane_Gonzalo%2C_St_Martin.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Orient_Beach_after_Hurricane_Gonzalo%2C_St_Martin.jpg/440px-Orient_Beach_after_Hurricane_Gonzalo%2C_St_Martin.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4928" data-file-height="3264" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Orient_Beach_after_Hurricane_Gonzalo,_St_Martin.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Damage to coastal installations at Orient Beach on <a href="/wiki/Saint_Martin_(island)" title="Saint Martin (island)">Saint Martin</a></div></div></div>
<p>The storm wrecked dozens of boats around Saint Martin, including 22 in <a href="/wiki/Simpson_Bay_Lagoon" title="Simpson Bay Lagoon">Simpson Bay Lagoon</a>, and an elderly man aboard one of the stricken vessels was killed. Two people on Saint Martin and <a href="/wiki/Saint_Barth%C3%A9lemy" title="Saint Barthélemy">Saint Barthélemy</a> went missing in the storm,<sup id="cite_ref-martin_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-martin-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> and were presumed dead after remaining unaccounted for several months later.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Fourteen other missing individuals were returned alive.<sup id="cite_ref-week_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-week-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Winds on Saint Barthélemy blew 86&#160;mph (138&#160;km/h) sustained, with gusts to 126&#160;mph (203&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Fallen trees obstructed streets, and an aircraft flipped over on a runway at <a href="/wiki/Gustaf_III_Airport" title="Gustaf III Airport">Gustaf III Airport</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-barts_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-barts-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> As many as 40 boats were reportedly beached on Saint Barthélemy.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On Saint Martin, sustained winds exceeded 60 mph (95 km/h), and <a href="/wiki/L%27Esp%C3%A9rance_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="L&#39;Espérance Airport">L'Espérance Airport</a> recorded gusts to 93&#160;mph (150&#160;km/h) before the observing equipment failed. As much as 5.70&#160;in (145&#160;mm) of rain fell over the island.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Reports of urban flooding and entrance of water into homes were common.<sup id="cite_ref-kitts_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kitts-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Gonzalo impaired water and electricity services throughout Dutch Sint Marteen and inflicted significant damage to homes. The St. Maarten Zoo sustained heavy structural damage, though all resident animals survived unharmed.<sup id="cite_ref-martin_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-martin-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> The French side of the island incurred relatively little destruction, with scattered roof and window damage,<sup id="cite_ref-kitts_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kitts-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> though Orient Beach "was a scene of complete devastation"; several businesses there suffered extensively.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Emergency responders and military aircraft from <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a> were dispatched to aid in post-storm recovery on Saint Barthélemy and Saint Martin.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Heavy rain on Anguilla flooded the <a href="/wiki/Clayton_J._Lloyd_International_Airport" title="Clayton J. Lloyd International Airport">Clayton J. Lloyd International Airport</a> and portions of several <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Anguilla" class="mw-redirect" title="Districts of Anguilla">districts</a>, qualifying the government for a US$500,000 "excess rainfall" insurance payout.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> The eastern and western ends of the island bore the brunt of the storm, facing damage to utility poles, vegetation, and roofs,<sup id="cite_ref-ccrif_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccrif-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> and public schools did not reopen until October 20 or later.<sup id="cite_ref-intact_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intact-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> The sole hospital experienced minor flood damage.<sup id="cite_ref-ccrif_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccrif-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Gonzalo produced squally weather in parts of Puerto Rico and the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Islands" title="Virgin Islands">Virgin Islands</a>, causing power outages and prompting 20 people to stay in an emergency shelter.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fema_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fema-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Cyril_E._King_Airport" title="Cyril E. King Airport">Cyril E. King Airport</a> on <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas,_U.S._Virgin_Islands" title="Saint Thomas, U.S. Virgin Islands">Saint Thomas</a> was temporarily closed due to the storm;<sup id="cite_ref-fema_53-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fema-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> the airport endured blustery conditions, with gusts reaching 35&#160;mph (56&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> Elsewhere, rough seas affected parts of the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Antilles" title="Greater Antilles">Greater Antilles</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Bahamas" class="mw-redirect" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pr_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bermuda">Bermuda</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Gonzalo_approaching_and_passing_Bermuda.gif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Hurricane_Gonzalo_approaching_and_passing_Bermuda.gif/250px-Hurricane_Gonzalo_approaching_and_passing_Bermuda.gif" decoding="async" width="250" height="167" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Hurricane_Gonzalo_approaching_and_passing_Bermuda.gif/375px-Hurricane_Gonzalo_approaching_and_passing_Bermuda.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Hurricane_Gonzalo_approaching_and_passing_Bermuda.gif/500px-Hurricane_Gonzalo_approaching_and_passing_Bermuda.gif 2x" data-file-width="720" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Gonzalo_approaching_and_passing_Bermuda.gif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Infrared satellite loop of Gonzalo passing through Bermuda on October<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>18</div></div></div>
<p>Gonzalo generated hurricane-force winds across Bermuda over a period of about six hours, at their strongest from the northwest on the backside of the storm.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Tropical storm conditions persisted for up to 24 hours.<sup id="cite_ref-raiv_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-raiv-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Elevated weather stations observed the highest winds; Commissioner's Point recorded 10-minute sustained winds of 109&#160;mph (175&#160;km/h), and St. David's measured gusts as high as 144&#160;mph (232&#160;km/h). Gusts reached 113&#160;mph (182&#160;km/h) at the Causeway, closer to sea level.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The airport recorded 2.85&#160;in (72&#160;mm) of rain, but due to the strong winds, this was most likely an underestimation of what actually fell. The passage of the hurricane's eye was marked by diminished winds and a lack of rain, but a drizzle of ocean mist reportedly fell over the island.<sup id="cite_ref-raiv_56-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-raiv-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> As Gonzalo struck at low tide, the effects of storm surge were minimal. At Esso Pier on the north side of the island, storm tides peaked at 3.25&#160;ft (0.99&#160;m) above normal.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-raiv_56-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-raiv-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The intense winds brought down utility poles and hundreds of trees (likely exacerbated by saturated ground from record rainfall in the months prior), leaving "barely a road" passable.<sup id="cite_ref-raiv_56-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-raiv-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-blocked_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blocked-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-species_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-species-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> The storm razed several invasive species, with endemic trees proving more resilient.<sup id="cite_ref-species_58-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-species-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> A composting facility received 1,200 truckloads of plant debris per day after Fay and Gonzalo, up from an average of 100.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> Damage from the storms totaled around US$260,000 on <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_National_Trust" title="Bermuda National Trust">Bermuda National Trust</a> properties, which include nature preserves and cemeteries.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> Similarly, the <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_Botanical_Gardens" title="Bermuda Botanical Gardens">Bermuda Botanical Gardens</a> and Arboretum were closed due to safety hazards resulting from extensive vegetation damage.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton,_Bermuda_(15585769125).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Hamilton%2C_Bermuda_%2815585769125%29.jpg/220px-Hamilton%2C_Bermuda_%2815585769125%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Hamilton%2C_Bermuda_%2815585769125%29.jpg/330px-Hamilton%2C_Bermuda_%2815585769125%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Hamilton%2C_Bermuda_%2815585769125%29.jpg/440px-Hamilton%2C_Bermuda_%2815585769125%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5312" data-file-height="2988" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton,_Bermuda_(15585769125).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Downed trees in <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_Bermuda" title="Hamilton, Bermuda">Hamilton, Bermuda</a> after Gonzalo passed by</div></div></div>
<p>The first storm-related power outages took place in the early afternoon on October 17 as weather conditions worsened.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> At the height of the storm, about 31,000 out of 36,000 electricity customers were without power,<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> including the 1,500 outages left unresolved after Fay.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> Backup CARILEC crews helped with specialty assignments, such as commercial outages and homes without power since before Gonzalo's onslaught.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> Service was not fully restored until November 3,<sup id="cite_ref-raiv_56-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-raiv-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> and BELCO ultimately spent US$2.9<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>million on system repairs after hurricanes Fay and Gonzalo. The company replaced 228 utility poles, 80 <a href="/wiki/Transformer" title="Transformer">transformers</a>, and over four miles (6.5 km) of wire.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> Other utilities, such as television and Internet services, also suffered, and technicians handled individual outages deep into the month of November.<sup id="cite_ref-raiv_56-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-raiv-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> About six hundred streetlights on main roads and several hundred more on side-streets were inoperative following the hurricane; some remained unmended through January 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Structural damage ranged from the superficial to complete wall or roof failure, the latter being relatively uncommon.<sup id="cite_ref-raiv_56-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-raiv-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> A multitude of buildings, including churches, a visitor's center, the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Assembly_of_Bermuda" title="House of Assembly of Bermuda">House of Assembly</a> building in Hamilton, and the Bermuda Police Service headquarters at <a href="/wiki/Prospect_Camp,_Bermuda" title="Prospect Camp, Bermuda">Prospect Camp</a>, suffered some degree of roof damage.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-house_71-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-house-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-reopenroof_72-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reopenroof-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> Older structures were particularly prone to substantial damage,<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> as were commercial storage buildings subject to less strict building codes.<sup id="cite_ref-ceo_73-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ceo-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup> In December, five new slate quarries were approved to accommodate the demand for roofing materials.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup> Both the new and the old portions of King Edward<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>VII Memorial Hospital received significant damage that exposed the facilities to the elements.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup> An exhibit at the <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_Aquarium,_Museum_and_Zoo" title="Bermuda Aquarium, Museum and Zoo">Bermuda Aquarium, Museum and Zoo</a> was unroofed, and coastal erosion threatened to encroach on the site, though no animals were harmed.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup> Part of the structure's roof was blown a quarter mile (400 m).<sup id="cite_ref-property_77-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-property-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>L.F. Wade International Airport sustained some roof and runway lighting damage,<sup id="cite_ref-airport_78-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-airport-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_Weather_Service" title="Bermuda Weather Service">Bermuda Weather Service</a> building lost a storm shutter and saw water forced inside a communications room.<sup id="cite_ref-raiv_56-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-raiv-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> A restaurant at the site of the <a href="/wiki/Gibbs_Hill_Lighthouse" title="Gibbs Hill Lighthouse">Gibbs Hill Lighthouse</a> was severely damaged, requiring a complete roof replacement.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">&#91;80&#93;</a></sup> The Causeway was largely spared, with some damage to the safety walls alongside the road, and it was partially reopened on October 18 after initial repairs.<sup id="cite_ref-reopenroof_72-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reopenroof-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> However, one of two lanes remained shut down for several days.<sup id="cite_ref-raiv_56-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-raiv-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Many boats were washed ashore and damaged or wrecked by the storm,<sup id="cite_ref-blocked_57-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blocked-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> and other vessels tipped over in boatyards.<sup id="cite_ref-property_77-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-property-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup> According to preliminary estimates, properties in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy_Dockyard" title="Royal Navy Dockyard">Dockyard</a> alone incurred US$1<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>million (2014 <a href="/wiki/Bermudian_dollar" title="Bermudian dollar">BMD</a>) in damage.<sup id="cite_ref-house_71-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-house-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Royal_Navy_Type_23_Frigate_HMS_Argyll_MOD_45153839.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Royal_Navy_Type_23_Frigate_HMS_Argyll_MOD_45153839.jpg/220px-Royal_Navy_Type_23_Frigate_HMS_Argyll_MOD_45153839.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="176" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Royal_Navy_Type_23_Frigate_HMS_Argyll_MOD_45153839.jpg/330px-Royal_Navy_Type_23_Frigate_HMS_Argyll_MOD_45153839.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Royal_Navy_Type_23_Frigate_HMS_Argyll_MOD_45153839.jpg/440px-Royal_Navy_Type_23_Frigate_HMS_Argyll_MOD_45153839.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2473" data-file-height="1979" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Royal_Navy_Type_23_Frigate_HMS_Argyll_MOD_45153839.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/HMS_Argyll_(F231)" title="HMS Argyll (F231)">HMS&#160;<i>Argyll</i></a> traveled to Bermuda from the Caribbean to aid in recovery efforts.</div></div></div>
<p>The strongest hurricane to directly affect Bermuda since 2003's <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Fabian" title="Hurricane Fabian">Hurricane Fabian</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Gonzalo was generally less destructive.<sup id="cite_ref-fabian_81-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fabian-81">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup> Damage modelling firms estimated insured losses from the hurricane to settle between US$200<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>million and US$400<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>million, not including damage to watercraft,<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">&#91;82&#93;</a></sup> though the CEO of the largest property insurer on Bermuda believed losses to be much lower.<sup id="cite_ref-ceo_73-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ceo-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup> It was estimated that a Fabian equivalence in 2014 would cause about US$650 million in damage.<sup id="cite_ref-air_83-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-air-83">&#91;83&#93;</a></sup> Some insurance companies decided to treat Fay and Gonzalo as a single event, allowing one deductible to count toward claims from both storms.<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">&#91;84&#93;</a></sup> After initial assessments, Premier Dunkley reported that the territory fared "much better than we expected",<sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">&#91;85&#93;</a></sup> and the storm was not blamed for any deaths or major injuries on Bermuda.<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">&#91;86&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the aftermath of the hurricane, a Royal Navy helicopter aboard <i>Argyll</i> flew out ahead of the ship to start aerial damage assessments. Members of the Bermuda Regiment immediately began cleanup and recovery efforts,<sup id="cite_ref-reopen_87-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reopen-87">&#91;87&#93;</a></sup> while the government provided <a href="/wiki/Tarpaulin" title="Tarpaulin">tarps</a> to affected homeowners.<sup id="cite_ref-airport_78-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-airport-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup> Sailors from the <i>Argyll</i> teamed up with Regiment soldiers upon the ship's arrival to continue relief work.<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">&#91;88&#93;</a></sup> Some 35 of the vessel's crew worked to clear debris from the runway at the airport, which reopened on October 19 after thorough evaluations.<sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89">&#91;89&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">&#91;90&#93;</a></sup> Including damage from Fay, about $2 million was spent on airport repairs, and the storms were later cited as evidence of the need for a newer terminal in a more protected location.<sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">&#91;91&#93;</a></sup> All schools on the territory resumed classes by October 21.<sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92">&#91;92&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atlantic_Canada">Atlantic Canada</span></h3>
<p>On October 17, the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hurricane_Centre" title="Canadian Hurricane Centre">Canadian Hurricane Centre</a> issued a tropical storm watch for the Avalon Peninsula of <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a>, between <a href="/wiki/Arnold%27s_Cove" title="Arnold&#39;s Cove">Arnold's Cove</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harbour_Main-Chapel%27s_Cove-Lakeview" title="Harbour Main-Chapel&#39;s Cove-Lakeview">Chapel's Cove</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-93" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-93">&#91;93&#93;</a></sup> Some communities, including <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="St. John&#39;s, Newfoundland and Labrador">St. John's</a>, worked to clear debris from culverts and storm drains to minimize the effects of flooding.<sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94">&#91;94&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-canada_95-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-canada-95">&#91;95&#93;</a></sup> Outer rainbands produced up to 2.7&#160;in (69&#160;mm) of rain in just a few hours,<sup id="cite_ref-chc_96-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chc-96">&#91;96&#93;</a></sup> which produced localized urban flooding in St. John's.<sup id="cite_ref-canada_95-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-canada-95">&#91;95&#93;</a></sup> Winds gusted to 66&#160;mph (106&#160;km/h) at <a href="/wiki/Cape_Pine" title="Cape Pine">Cape Pine</a> and 55&#160;mph (89&#160;km/h) at Cape Race.<sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97">&#91;97&#93;</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s_International_Airport" title="St. John&#39;s International Airport">St. John's International Airport</a>, sustained winds of 34 mph (54 km/h) were punctuated by gusts to 46&#160;mph (74&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-chc_96-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chc-96">&#91;96&#93;</a></sup> The winds briefly cut power to about 100 households in a St. John's neighborhood.<sup id="cite_ref-canada_95-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-canada-95">&#91;95&#93;</a></sup> Offshore, a buoy over the <a href="/wiki/Laurentian_fan" title="Laurentian fan">Laurentian fan</a> recorded peak wave heights to 68&#160;ft (21&#160;m),<sup id="cite_ref-chc_96-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chc-96">&#91;96&#93;</a></sup> and an oil rig southeast of the storm's center experienced sustained winds of 98&#160;mph (158&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-disc29_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-disc29-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Low astronomical tides prevented significant coastal flooding, but a 2.6<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>ft (0.8<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>m) surge was recorded at both St. John's and <a href="/wiki/Argentia" title="Argentia">Argentia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-chc_96-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chc-96">&#91;96&#93;</a></sup> Farther west, the hurricane generated rough seas and rip currents along the Atlantic coast of <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-98" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-98">&#91;98&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom_and_Europe">United Kingdom and Europe</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Elgol_on_Skye_(15761067344).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Elgol_on_Skye_%2815761067344%29.jpg/220px-Elgol_on_Skye_%2815761067344%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Elgol_on_Skye_%2815761067344%29.jpg/330px-Elgol_on_Skye_%2815761067344%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Elgol_on_Skye_%2815761067344%29.jpg/440px-Elgol_on_Skye_%2815761067344%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2539" data-file-height="1682" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Elgol_on_Skye_(15761067344).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Strong waves from Gonzalo hit the coast of the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Skye" title="Isle of Skye">Isle of Skye</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The extratropical storm complex incorporating the remnants of Gonzalo generated strong winds across the British Isles, especially over Ireland and the northern United Kingdom. Winds gusted to 88&#160;mph (142&#160;km/h) at <a href="/wiki/Oban" title="Oban">Oban</a> in western Scotland,<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> while gusts exceeding 60 mph (100 km/h) were common elsewhere.<sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99">&#91;99&#93;</a></sup> The system halted transportation throughout the region, forcing the cancellation of more than a hundred flights at <a href="/wiki/London_Heathrow_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="London Heathrow Airport">London Heathrow Airport</a>, grounding ferries, and blocking roads and railways with debris.<sup id="cite_ref-tcr_2-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Falling trees killed one woman near <a href="/wiki/Hyde_Park,_London" title="Hyde Park, London">Hyde Park</a>, London, and injured several others.<sup id="cite_ref-100" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-100">&#91;100&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a>, the winds collapsed a <a href="/wiki/Jack_(device)" title="Jack (device)">car jack</a> supporting a van, fatally crushing a man working underneath.<sup id="cite_ref-tele_101-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tele-101">&#91;101&#93;</a></sup> The storm triggered minor power outages in Scotland and Wales,<sup id="cite_ref-102" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-102">&#91;102&#93;</a></sup> with more extensive outages in northwestern Ireland after gusts as high as 115&#160;km/h (71&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-103" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-103">&#91;103&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:%C3%9Cberflutetes_Elbufer_Hamburg-Rissen_-_Orkan_Gonzalo_(22.10.2014)_03.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/%C3%9Cberflutetes_Elbufer_Hamburg-Rissen_-_Orkan_Gonzalo_%2822.10.2014%29_03.jpg/220px-%C3%9Cberflutetes_Elbufer_Hamburg-Rissen_-_Orkan_Gonzalo_%2822.10.2014%29_03.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/%C3%9Cberflutetes_Elbufer_Hamburg-Rissen_-_Orkan_Gonzalo_%2822.10.2014%29_03.jpg/330px-%C3%9Cberflutetes_Elbufer_Hamburg-Rissen_-_Orkan_Gonzalo_%2822.10.2014%29_03.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/%C3%9Cberflutetes_Elbufer_Hamburg-Rissen_-_Orkan_Gonzalo_%2822.10.2014%29_03.jpg/440px-%C3%9Cberflutetes_Elbufer_Hamburg-Rissen_-_Orkan_Gonzalo_%2822.10.2014%29_03.jpg 2x" data-file-width="6000" data-file-height="4000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:%C3%9Cberflutetes_Elbufer_Hamburg-Rissen_-_Orkan_Gonzalo_(22.10.2014)_03.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Flooding along the <a href="/wiki/Elbe" title="Elbe">Elbe River</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rissen" title="Rissen">Hamburg-Rissen</a>, Germany, on October<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>22</div></div></div>
<p>Western and central Europe were also impacted by damaging winds and downpours. The coast of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> endured high seas and gusts to 108&#160;km/h (67&#160;mph), uprooting trees and flooding the coastline. Rail services and flight operations were disrupted throughout the region.<sup id="cite_ref-netherlands_104-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-netherlands-104">&#91;104&#93;</a></sup> In Germany, gusts over 145&#160;km/h (90&#160;mph) were recorded at high elevations.<sup id="cite_ref-105" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-105">&#91;105&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart" title="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a> alone, fifty vehicles were damaged by falling debris;<sup id="cite_ref-germany_106-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-germany-106">&#91;106&#93;</a></sup> downed trees, torn roofs, and power outages were common throughout the nation.<sup id="cite_ref-107" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-107">&#91;107&#93;</a></sup> In the Bavarian capital of <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>, the storm destroyed a large tin roof measuring over 80&#160;m<sup>2</sup> (860&#160;sq&#160;ft), parts of which impacted cars and fences on the ground. Damage from the incident was estimated at €500,000 (US$637,000).<sup id="cite_ref-108" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-108">&#91;108&#93;</a></sup> Throughout Germany, the storm wrought €60–100 million (US$80–130 million) in insured losses. A woman died in a traffic accident, attributed to wet road conditions, along the <a href="/wiki/Bundesstra%C3%9Fe_303" title="Bundesstraße 303">Bundesstraße 303</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bayreuth_(district)" title="Bayreuth (district)">Bayreuth district</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-109" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-109">&#91;109&#93;</a></sup> Northwesterly gales yielded a storm surge along the country's North Sea coastline,<sup id="cite_ref-110" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-110">&#91;110&#93;</a></sup> with tides running over 2&#160;m (6&#160;ft 7&#160;in) above normal along the <a href="/wiki/Elbe" title="Elbe">Elbe River</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a>. Neighboring Austria and Switzerland also experienced gales and power outages.<sup id="cite_ref-germany_106-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-germany-106">&#91;106&#93;</a></sup> A tornado touched down near <a href="/wiki/Neukirchen_an_der_Enknach" title="Neukirchen an der Enknach">Neukirchen an der Enknach</a> in Austria, damaging homes and farms.<sup id="cite_ref-111" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-111">&#91;111&#93;</a></sup> Gusts in southern Switzerland reached 185&#160;km/h (115&#160;mph), leading to road and rail closures.<sup id="cite_ref-112" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-112">&#91;112&#93;</a></sup> In southern France, the system initiated a <a href="/wiki/Mistral_(wind)" title="Mistral (wind)">mistral wind</a> event.<sup id="cite_ref-germany_106-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-germany-106">&#91;106&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The upper-level low associated with the hurricane's remnants blanketed parts of the <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">Alps</a> with heavy snowfall, accumulating to several feet,<sup id="cite_ref-germany_106-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-germany-106">&#91;106&#93;</a></sup> and subsequently fueled torrential rains in the <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkans</a> on October 24 and 25. Floodwaters caused by estimated rainfall totals nearing 150&#160;mm (6&#160;in) inundated entire villages in southeastern Bulgaria, forcing hundreds to evacuate, and there were widespread disruptions of power and water services. A woman in <a href="/wiki/Burgas" title="Burgas">Burgas</a> died from drowning. In the Greek capital city of <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>, the deluge filled streets with rushing water that swept away hundreds of vehicles.<sup id="cite_ref-113" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-113">&#91;113&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-114" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-114">&#91;114&#93;</a></sup> More than three hundred buildings were inundated in the <a href="/wiki/Attica" title="Attica">Attica</a> area.<sup id="cite_ref-115" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-115">&#91;115&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
